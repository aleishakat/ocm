Aleisha Djuricic  |  Organizational Change Management

Technical communications professional in SE Wisconsin, specializing in organizational change management, communications, and technology adoption and training.

Let's face it, not everyone is excited about embracing technology changes. Whether your organization feels ahead of the adoption curve, or lagging way behind, it's difficult to get busy users to spend the time and energy needed to truly adopt a new technology. Even users who consider themselves tech-savvy need a little help sometimes. 

 I create and deliver approaches that maximize the ability for end-users to adopt new technologies and adapt to changing ones. I often consider myself a "technology therapist" who helps users adapt to the initial discomfort of the change, learn how to effectively use the technology and then find ways to boost their productivity.

Skype Migration 

CHALLENGE
Imagine you have to tell more than 6,000 employees that you are replacing their beloved desk phones with a Vo/IP telephone, changing how voicemail messaging works and impacting hundreds of processes that have been in place for years.

RESULTS
On this large program, I was responsible for developing and executing a training and adoption strategy for Skype for Business. We wanted to ensure that all learners (6,200+) received a just-in-time training with Skype for Business, Unified Messaging and new video-conferencing technologies. 

  • Masterminded the deployment strategy to migrate 6,200 users to Skype for Business and Unified Communications, which included:
    • 100+ weekly migrations, with less than .05% service tickets logged. (That's less than 1 ticket per 100 users!)
    • Provided a multi-channel organizational change and training strategy.
    • Part of the team leading the effort for technology kiosks to introduce the new technology to users. This concept has been carried forward and is still used today.

Contact Center Software Migration 

CHALLENGE
Develop and Deliver Training to 1,200+ Contact Center Users
RESULTS 
Developed a training strategy that allowed a "train-the-trainer" approach and provided all users with multiple opportunities to learn, with a primary focus on instructor-led training and hands-on practice.

Designed turn-key course materials that allowed 8 instructors to train more than 1,200 contact center users to Customer Interaction Center software (Genesys/Interactive Intelligence).
  • Training and adoption activities went on for almost a year
  • 50+ weekly migrations to the new contact center system
  • 400+ call queues across more than 30 business areas
